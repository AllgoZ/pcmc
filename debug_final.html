Numbers

A number is an arithmetic value used to represent quantity. Hence, a number is a mathematical concept used to count, measure, and label. Thus, numbers form the basis of mathematics. It is the mathematical notation for representing numbers and provides a unique representation of every number and represents the arithmetic and algebraic structure of the figures. In this lesson we will learn about Rational numbers and their properties, Compute the Square, Square root, Cube and Cube root of numbers, Exponential form and express the numbers in Scientific notation.

Measurements

Measurement is a process of determining how large or small a physical quantity is as compared to a basic reference quantity of the same kind. Measurement refers to the comparison of an unknown quantity with a known quantity. The result of a measurement is a numeric value with certain units. We can measure the length, mass, capacity (volume), and temperature of any given object. In this lesson we will learn about the Parts of a circle, To calculate the length of arc, Area and Circumference of a sector, Area and the Perimeter of combined plane figures, Representation of 3-D shapes in 2-D and 3-D objects with Cubes.

Algebra

Algebra is the branch of mathematics in which abstract symbols, rather than numbers, are manipulated or operated with arithmetic. It is used mathematical statements to describe relationships between things that vary. In this lesson we will learn about Multiplication and Division of algebraic expressions and algebraic identities, Solve word problems in linear equations, How to plot the points in the graph and draw graphs of simple linear equations.

Life Mathematics

Life mathematics, also known as actuarial mathematics, involves using mathematical and statistical methods to assess risk in the insurance and finance industries. Some common formulas used in life mathematics include those for calculating present value, future value, annuities, and mortality rates. In this lesson we will learn about find the compound interest through patterns and formula and use them in simple problems. compound variation and solve time and work problems.

Geometry

Geometry is a branch of maths that deals with points, lines, shapes, and surfaces. Simply defined as Geometry is the study of shapes. In this lesson we will learn about the Pythagorean theorem and solve problems using it, Concurrency of Medians, Altitudes, Angle bisectors and perpendicular bisectors in a Triangle, Construct Quadrilaterals of various types.

Statistics

Basic statistics concepts refer to the fundamental elements of statistics. Statistics is the science concerned with developing and studying methods for Collecting, Analyzing, Interpreting and Presenting empirical data. It helps in gathering information about the appropriate quantitative data. In this lesson we will learn about Frequency Tables, Construct simple Pie-charts for the given data and how to draw Histogram and Frequency Polygon for grouped data.

Information Processing

Information processing relates to the ways in which individuals make sense of, or interpret, the information to which they are exposed. Success in Mathematics depends on the development of the number sense, logical thinking and cognitive skills. In this lesson we will learn about the number of possible orderings of an arbitrary number of objects, SET game, Fibonacci pattern, Finding the HCF of numbers, How information can be processed in Encryption and Decryption and Shopping ideas.
